Chapter 1: The Call

The corgi in Recovery Room B has had a very expensive day and won’t stop screaming about it.

Not literally screaming—veterinary screaming, which is more of a sustained, indignant wheeze that somehow carries the emotional weight of a toddler in a grocery store. I know because I’ve heard both, and the corgi is honestly winning on conviction.

“His gums are perfect,” I say to Priya, who’s pretending not to notice that I’m narrating my own life like someone who spent too long on the internet. “No secondary infection. Dr. Chen’s work is clean.”

Chapter 2: The Pitch

The blank Google Doc has been blank for forty-five minutes, which is a personal record. I’ve stared at blank pages before—plenty of them, increasingly, over the last six months—but there was usually at least the ghost of an idea somewhere in the white space. A headline hovering like a half-remembered dream. A phrase that might become something if I let it sit long enough.

This time there’s nothing. The cursor blinks at me like an accusation.

Chapter 3: The Negotiation

Chapter 3: The Negotiation

I wake up to pins and needles and the smell of coffee.

The first part makes sense—Captain has somehow in the night migrated from being across my legs to being fully on top of my right leg, all 140 pounds of him, which is an anatomically ambitious position and absolutely cutting off circulation. I extract myself carefully, trying not to wake him, which doesn’t work. Captain opens one eye, assesses my crime of leaving, and puts his head back down with a heavy sigh that suggests he’s very disappointed in my priorities.

Chapter 5: The Routine

Chapter 5: The Routine

By Wednesday of the second week, the mornings have a rhythm.

I’m at June’s by 7 AM, keys in the decorative stone, coffee already made because Eli apparently wakes up early and doesn’t know what to do with the quiet. Captain hears my car and is waiting by the back door, tail going but controlled. He’s better on walks now — still nervous about plastic bags and leaf-blowers and the general chaos of the world outside, but functional. We loop the block. He sniffs things. Sometimes he pulls toward something interesting. It’s almost normal.

Chapter 6: The Notebook

The days have shape now.

Wake at 7, shower, walk to June’s. Three doors down, two blocks over. Close enough that I could do it in my sleep, far enough that it feels like going somewhere. The walk matters. It separates Kat’s house (where I sleep, where I fail to write, where my sister looks at me like an X-ray) from June’s house (where Captain waits, where there’s a routine, where I have a reason to exist).
