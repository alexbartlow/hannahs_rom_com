# The Worst Haiku You've Ever Heard
### A Rom-Com Novella

---

## Premise

**Nora Castillo** (29, veterinary technician) and **Eli Barker** (31, recently laid-off copywriter turned reluctant dog-sitter) are forced into each other's orbits when Nora's elderly neighbor, **June Matsuda**, breaks her hip and begs them — separately — to co-foster her enormous, anxious Great Dane, **Captain**.

The problem: Captain has separation anxiety so severe he can't be alone for more than twenty minutes without destroying furniture. Nora works days. Eli, jobless and crashing in his sister's guest room two doors down from June's house, has nothing but time. They need each other. They do not want to need each other.

Nora is warm, competent, and funny, but she's been so focused on being the reliable one in every relationship that she's forgotten how to let anyone take care of *her*. Eli is dry, self-deprecating, and quietly brilliant, but he's spiraling after losing the career he built his identity around — and he masks it with detachment and terrible poetry.

Captain, for his part, is 140 pounds of anxious velvet who sits on people's laps and is afraid of the toaster.

---

## Point of View & Structure

- **Alternating dual first-person POV** — Nora odd chapters, Eli even chapters
- Each of Eli's chapters ends with a bad haiku recapping his emotional state
- ~24 chapters + epilogue
- Target: ~40,000 words (~1,500-1,800 words per chapter)

---

## Cast

**Nora Castillo** — Vet tech at a small-animal clinic. Puerto Rican, grew up in a loud, loving, slightly overbearing family. Her last relationship ended because her ex said she was "too much" — too opinionated, too intense, too involved. She's internalized that more than she admits. Copes by being indispensable to everyone around her. Default mode: competent and cheerful. Hidden mode: lonely and afraid that "too much" is just... what she is.

**Eli Barker** — Former senior copywriter at a mid-tier ad agency, laid off in a round of cuts six months ago. White, from a family of high achievers (sister is a surgeon, dad was an engineer). He was good at his job and liked it, which makes losing it feel like losing the one thing he contributed. He's been freelancing but can't land anything. Writes poetry in a notebook he'd die before showing anyone. The poetry is objectively terrible. He knows this. He keeps writing anyway. Default mode: wry and low-key. Hidden mode: terrified he's not enough.

**Captain** — June's Great Dane. Blue merle, enormous, gentle, neurotic. Afraid of: the toaster, plastic bags, the UPS truck, and being alone. Loves: laps (despite being 140 lbs), Nora's voice, Eli's calm energy, and a specific squeaky toy shaped like a taco. Captain is the emotional barometer of the book — when he's anxious, the leads are avoiding something. When he's settled, they're being honest.

**June Matsuda** — 74, retired high school English teacher. Sharp, meddlesome, loving. She absolutely engineered the co-fostering arrangement knowing full well what she was doing. Calls Eli "dear" and Nora "mija" (Nora taught her). Recovering from hip surgery at her daughter's house across town.

**Kat Barker-Okafor** — Eli's older sister. Orthopedic surgeon. Married to **Dayo**. Lives two doors down from June. Loves her brother, worries about him, shows it by being slightly too direct. She's the one who told June that Eli needed a project.

**The Castillo Family** — Nora's mom **Alma** (texts constantly, sends unsolicited novenas), her dad **Luis** (quieter, sends Nora articles about dog behavior he finds on Facebook), her younger brother **Marco** (23, chaotic, borrows things and doesn't return them).

---

## Emotional Arc

### Nora's Arc
**From:** "I have to be everything for everyone, and I can't let anyone see me struggle."
**To:** "Being loved isn't a reward for being useful. I can just... be."

### Eli's Arc
**From:** "I lost the only thing that made me valuable, and I'm faking my way through every day."
**To:** "My worth isn't my output. And the things I make just for myself — the weird, bad, embarrassing things — those count too."

### Relationship Arc
**From:** Reluctant co-parents of a neurotic dog
**Through:** Grudging respect → genuine friendship → mutual vulnerability → the moment they both realize → avoidance because the stakes feel too high → honest reckoning
**To:** Choosing each other, not because they need each other for Captain logistics, but because they just... want to be in each other's lives.

## Act Structure

### ACT ONE: The Arrangement (Chapters 1-8)
*Establishing the world, the problem, and why these two people are stuck together.*

**Chapter 1 — Nora** | *"The Call"*
Nora gets a frantic call from June's daughter: June fell, broke her hip, is in surgery. Can Nora check on Captain? Nora races over. Captain is a wreck — shaking, won't eat, has pulled a cushion off the couch. Nora calms him down, stays the night. Establishes: Nora's competence, her relationship with June, her tenderness with animals, the hint that taking care of others is where she feels safe.

**Chapter 2 — Eli** | *"The Pitch"*
Eli is at Kat's kitchen table, pretending to work on freelance pitches that don't exist. Kat tells him June's in the hospital and someone needs to help with the dog. Eli resists. Kat says: "You need a reason to leave this house, Eli." He goes to June's. Captain is there. So is a woman he's never met, asleep on June's couch with the dog draped across her legs. *Haiku: "Dog on a stranger / I came here to help someone / The couch is taken"*

**Chapter 3 — Nora** | *"The Negotiation"*
Nora wakes up to a strange man in June's kitchen making coffee. Awkward introductions. They discover June has separately asked them both to take care of Captain. Nora suggests they figure out a schedule. Eli is clearly out of his depth with the dog. Nora is slightly patronizing about it. Eli notices.

**Chapter 4 — Eli** | *"The Handoff"*
First day of the co-fostering schedule. Eli has Captain during the day while Nora works. Captain destroys a shoe, sits on Eli's laptop, and refuses to go outside because a plastic bag blew across the yard. Eli calls June at rehab. June says: "He just needs someone calm. You're very calm, dear." Eli does not feel calm. But he sits on the floor with the dog and reads out loud from whatever's on his phone, and Captain settles. A small, unexpected competence. *Haiku: "Giant coward dog / You and me, afraid of bags / At least you're honest"*

**Chapter 5 — Nora** | *"The Routine"*
A week in. The handoff system is working. Nora comes to June's after work, Eli gives her a status report (increasingly detailed and funny — he's started texting her updates during the day). Nora notices he's good with Captain, actually. They have their first real conversation on June's porch while Captain lies between them. She learns he's a writer. He doesn't say he's unemployed; he says he's "freelancing." She talks about the clinic. Easy. Normal. She hasn't had a normal conversation with a man in months that wasn't about her being too much.

**Chapter 6 — Eli** | *"The Notebook"*
Eli is writing more. Captain's routine gives his days structure for the first time in months. He starts writing haikus about the dog as a joke. Then about the neighborhood. Then about the way Nora smells like antiseptic and lavender when she shows up for the evening handoff. He is alarmed by that last one. Texts Kat: "This is a professional dog co-parenting arrangement." Kat: "Nobody said it wasn't." *Haiku: "She smells like two things / One is clean, one is a flower / This is about dog"*

**Chapter 7 — Nora** | *"The Emergency"*
Captain eats something he shouldn't (a sock). Nora walks Eli through what to watch for, ends up coming over on her lunch break to check. She's in vet-tech mode, efficient and reassuring. Captain is fine. Eli watches her work and says, "You're really good at this." Not flirty. Just... factual. Nora doesn't know what to do with a compliment that isn't about her being helpful. It's about her being *skilled*. She thinks about it all afternoon.

**Chapter 8 — Eli** | *"The Dinner"*
Nora's mom Alma shows up at June's with food for "whoever is watching Capitán." She meets Eli. She immediately invites him to Sunday dinner. Eli says he couldn't possibly. Alma says it's not a question. Nora is mortified. Eli, to his own surprise, is charmed. *Haiku: "Her mother brought rice / and beans and an opinion / I think I said yes"*

---

### ACT TWO: The Friendship (Chapters 9-16)
*They become genuinely important to each other. The tension builds. They both find reasons not to name it.*

**Chapter 9 — Nora** | *"Sunday"*
Eli at Castillo Sunday dinner. He's quieter than her family but holds his own. Luis likes him. Marco tries to recruit him for a fantasy football league. Alma watches Nora watching Eli and says nothing, which is worse than saying something. Nora drives Eli home. In the car, he says, "Your family is really loud." She braces. He says, "I loved it." She doesn't know what to do with that either.

**Chapter 10 — Eli** | *"The Good Day"*
Eli lands a freelance gig — small, but real. He's disproportionately happy about it. Tells Captain first. Then texts Nora, and realizes she's the second person he wanted to tell, after a dog. Nora sends back a voice memo of her cheering. He listens to it twice. *Haiku: "I got paid to write / Someone wanted what I made / The dog doesn't care"*

**Chapter 11 — Nora** | *"The Crack"*
Nora has a bad day — difficult case at the clinic, an owner who was neglectful and belligerent. She shows up for the evening handoff and is clearly not okay but performing fine. Eli doesn't push. He just says, "I was going to order Thai. Stay?" She almost says no. Stays. Doesn't talk about it. But sits on the floor with Captain's head in her lap and lets herself be quiet for once. This is new for her.

**Chapter 12 — Eli** | *"The Almost"*
They're watching a movie at June's (ostensibly for Captain, who likes background noise). Nora falls asleep on the other end of the couch. Captain is between them. Eli watches her sleep for exactly two seconds before looking away because it feels like seeing something private. He covers her with a blanket. She wakes up an hour later, embarrassed. He says, "You needed it." She says, "I don't do that." He says, "You just did." *Haiku: "She fell asleep here / I did not touch her shoulder / I thought about it"*

**Chapter 13 — Nora** | *"The Question"*
June calls Nora from rehab. Recovering well, might be home in a few weeks. Nora realizes she doesn't want the arrangement to end. Not because of Captain — because of the porch, and the handoffs, and the texts during the day. She calls her friend **Priya** and says, "I think I have a problem." Priya says, "Is the problem that he's hot and nice and your dog loves him?" Nora says, "HE'S NOT MY DOG." Priya says, "Okay."

**Chapter 14 — Eli** | *"The Setback"*
The freelance client ghosts on payment. Eli spirals quietly — not about the money, but about what it confirms. He's short with Nora at the handoff. She notices. He deflects. She lets him, which is her pattern. He writes the worst haiku yet and almost throws the notebook away. *Haiku: "I am a grown man / Who cannot earn a living / Nice sunset, at least"*

**Chapter 15 — Nora** | *"The Fight"*
Nora finds out from Kat (at a neighborhood block party) that Eli has been unemployed for six months, not freelancing. She's not mad about the unemployment. She's mad that he hid it — because it reminds her of her ex, who hid things to manage her reactions, who treated her like someone who needed to be handled. She confronts Eli. It's not a screaming fight. It's a quiet, precise one. "You didn't have to tell me everything. But you chose to tell me something that wasn't true." Eli doesn't have a defense. She leaves. Captain whines at the door.

**Chapter 16 — Eli** | *"The Silence"*
Three days of minimal contact. They keep the Captain schedule because they're both responsible people, but the handoffs are efficient and brief. Eli sits with why he lied. It wasn't about managing her. It was about managing himself — if he said it out loud to someone he respected, it became real. He writes a haiku that's actually a little bit honest. *Haiku: "I lied about work / Not to fool you, but myself / That is somehow worse"*

---

### ACT THREE: The Reckoning (Chapters 17-24)
*The real obstacles surface. They deal with their own stuff. They choose each other.*

**Chapter 17 — Nora** | *"The Mirror"*
Nora talks to her mom about the fight (without specifics). Alma says: "Mija, not every man who doesn't tell you everything is treating you like you're too much. Some people are just ashamed." Nora sits with that. She also realizes she jumped to the worst interpretation because it was familiar, not because it was accurate. She's been so ready to be hurt the same way again that she almost manufactured it.

**Chapter 18 — Eli** | *"The Conversation"*
Eli goes to Nora. Not with a grand gesture. He shows up at the clinic parking lot at the end of her shift and says, "Can I talk to you?" He tells her the truth — all of it. The layoff, the spiral, the freelance drought, the way having Captain to take care of was the first thing in months that made him feel like he was doing something right. And that meeting her was the second thing. He doesn't ask for anything. He just wants her to have the real version. *Haiku: "I brought you the truth / It's smaller than I pretended / But it's all of it"*

**Chapter 19 — Nora** | *"The Thaw"*
They rebuild. Carefully. Nora lets him back in, but she also tells him her side — the ex, the "too much" thing, the way she pre-rejects to avoid being rejected. She's never said it that plainly before. Eli listens. Then says: "For the record, you're not too much. You're a lot. Those are different things." Captain puts his head on both their laps simultaneously, which is anatomically ambitious for even a Great Dane.

**Chapter 20 — Eli** | *"June Comes Home"*
June returns. She's delighted with the state of her house, her dog, and the obvious tension between her two fosters. She's not subtle about it. "You two should keep having dinner together. For Captain's sake." Eli and Nora both know what she's doing. Neither calls her on it. The co-fostering arrangement is technically over. They keep finding reasons to come over anyway. *Haiku: "The old woman smiles / Like she planned this from the start / She absolutely did"*

**Chapter 21 — Nora** | *"The Taco"*
Nora is at June's for dinner (she's been coming twice a week since June got home). Eli is there. Captain brings Nora his squeaky taco and puts it in her lap, which June says he's never done — he doesn't share the taco. Everyone in the room understands the significance except possibly Captain. Nora looks at Eli. Something shifts. Not dramatically. Just... settles. Like a decision she already made finally reaching her conscious mind.

**Chapter 22 — Eli** | *"The Porch"*
They're on June's porch after dinner. June has conveniently gone to bed early. Captain is lying across the top step like a furry roadblock. Eli says, "I wrote you something. It's bad. I want you to know that I know it's bad." He reads her a haiku. For the first time in the book, the haiku is actually good. (The reader should tear up. This is tear #4.) Nora laughs and then doesn't laugh. *Haiku: "You are the reason / I write anything at all / Even the bad ones"*

**Chapter 23 — Nora** | *"The Door"*
They kiss. On the porch. With Captain between them, tail going. Closed door. We don't follow them inside. We stay on the porch with the dog and the evening and the sound of the door closing behind them. (This is Nora's chapter, so we get her interiority up to the moment, and then the chapter ends with the click of the latch. The reader fills in the rest.)

**Chapter 24 — Eli** | *"The Morning"*
Morning. Eli is making coffee in June's kitchen. Nora comes in wearing his flannel. Captain is doing his whole-body tail wag. June, from the living room, without looking up from her crossword: "It's about time." Eli texts Kat: "I think I'm okay." Kat: "I know." No grand gesture. No public declaration. Just a Tuesday morning where everything is different and nothing is different and the coffee is ready and the dog is happy. *Haiku: "Tuesday. Coffee. You. / The dog is on both our feet / This is the whole point"*

---

### EPILOGUE — Nora | *"Six Months Later"*
Nora and Eli have their own place. Captain lives with June but stays with them on weekends (shared custody). Eli has a full-time content role he actually likes. Nora got promoted to lead tech. They go to Castillo Sunday dinner every week. Eli fits. On the fridge, held up by a magnet shaped like a taco, is a framed haiku — the porch one. Nora adds one underneath it in her handwriting. Hers is also terrible. It's perfect.

## Tear Budget Allocation (5 tears)

1. **Chapter 14** — Eli's quiet spiral. The haiku lands. (Tear of recognition.)
2. **Chapter 18** — Eli's honest confession in the parking lot. (Tear of vulnerability.)
3. **Chapter 19** — "You're not too much. You're a lot. Those are different things." (Tear of relief.)
4. **Chapter 22** — The good haiku. (Tear of earned emotion.)
5. **Chapter 24 or Epilogue** — The Tuesday morning / the fridge haiku. (Tear of satisfaction.)

## Tone & Style Notes

- **Funny first.** The humor should be in the voice — both POVs should make the reader smile on every page. Nora is drier than she appears. Eli is funnier than he thinks.
- **Internal monologue carries the comedy.** The situation is sweet; the voices make it funny.
- **Captain is never anthropomorphized beyond what's realistic.** He's a dog. He does dog things. The humans project onto him. That's the joke and the tenderness.
- **The haikus are genuinely bad** until Chapter 22. Syllable counts can be slightly off in the early ones (Eli trying too hard). They should make the reader snort.
- **Closed door.** Chemistry is built through: proximity, small gestures, noticing details, the way they talk to each other vs. how they talk to everyone else.
- **No villain.** Nora's ex is referenced but never appears. The obstacles are internal.
- **The ending is quiet.** No airport run, no rain-soaked confession. A kitchen and a dog and a Tuesday.
